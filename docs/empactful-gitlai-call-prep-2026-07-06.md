## 1. Read of the Room

Tim's package is friendly and well-prepared, and the comparison document is fair to GITLAI. But the framing is deliberate: **"the similarities in our approaches far exceed the differences"** is a convergence pitch, leading naturally to "let's fold these together." That is fine for delivery — and dangerous if it blurs into folding together the *assets*.

The comparison itself supplies the counter-frame, in Empactful's own words:

> "GITLAI is a lightweight, **vendor-neutral front door** that decides what to build before anything is chosen. Empactful Studios / actAVA is a heavier, **vendor-attached delivery engine** that turns an already-selected platform into a governed, compliant, ROI-instrumented deployment."

Those are not the same thing wearing different logos. They are two complementary pieces of one funnel — that's the thesis for the call.

**The most important thing in the package is what's missing:** the comparison covers *engagement models* only. The **repeatable engagement platform** — the thing Empactful wants built and Jason intends to build from GITLAI — appears nowhere in any of the four documents. That territory is unclaimed going into the call. Claim it.

## 2. The Combined Model to Propose

- **Two front doors.** actAVA-sourced deals enter through the ES/actAVA readiness track (platform already chosen). Everything else enters through the GITLAI Phase One Assessment (vendor-neutral — which may *conclude* actAVA, giving actAVA a second pipeline it doesn't currently have).
- **One delivery engine.** Empactful delivers the implementation track, with Jason contracted in for engagement #1.
- **One platform underneath both.** The repeatable engagement platform — engagement/phase/deliverable templates, scored instruments (workflow selection, readiness baseline, supervision designer, ROI builder), document generation — **built and owned by GITLAI, licensed to Empactful**, with actAVA as adapter #1 and vendor-neutral by architecture. This *is* Tim's "vendor neutral variant" ask from his original email: not a request, a solution to something he already asked for.

## 3. Positions to Hold

1. **Contract through GITLAI, LLC — not personally.** GITLAI's own example proposal (which Tim just circulated) already contains the exact clause: *"I retain ownership of my reusable frameworks, methods, and accelerators; the company receives a full license to the deliverables."* State that the same posture applies to the Empactful relationship — consistent, precedented, non-confrontational.
2. **Two agreements, not one:** (a) engagement-delivery services for the actAVA engagement; (b) platform development & licensing for the repeatable system. One contractor agreement must not govern both.
3. **Ownership question — name it, don't resolve it live.** Empactful's framing ("build a repeatable platform with Jason's help") and Jason's ("GITLAI delivers it for Empactful") quietly disagree. If it surfaces, name the three honest models — work-for-hire / GITLAI-product-licensed / **engine-instance hybrid (GITLAI owns the engine; Empactful owns its branded instance, methodology content, and client data)** — suggest the hybrid as the likely landing zone, and agree to term-sheet it rather than negotiate it on the call.
4. **Engagement #1 doubles as platform R&D.** Propose the instrument-capture discipline explicitly (capture every rubric, template, time log, and friction point as platform requirements) and that capture work is acknowledged Layer-2 scope, not silently absorbed into delivery.

## 6. Watch-Fors

- **"Similarities far exceed differences"** → merging approaches is fine; merging *ownership* is not. The similarity argument also cuts GITLAI's way: if the approaches are that similar, the vendor-neutral GITLAI version is the general case and the actAVA track is one instance of it.
- **Solo-vs-firm altitude framing.** The comparison repeatedly labels GITLAI "solo consultant" vs. Empactful "firm-level." Accurate for delivery capacity — but don't let it slide into "GITLAI is the subcontractor." GITLAI is solo as a consultancy; as a product owner it is the platform company in the room.
- **Nate is a new variable** — he owns the actAVA relationship. Anything agreed about methodology/IP must survive the actAVA conversation Nate has next.

## 7. Good Outcome for the Call

1. Agreement on the two-front-door / one-engine / one-platform model.
2. Agreement that a **platform term sheet** (ownership model, funding, license terms) gets drafted **before engagement #1 starts** — with a date attached.
3. Confirmation of first-hand prospect facts and the actAVA contracting structure (or an owner and a date for confirming them).
