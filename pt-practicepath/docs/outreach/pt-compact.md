# Outreach draft — PT Compact Commission (fee/roster data access)

**To:** PT Compact Commission (ptcompact.org — contact page)
**From:** Jason Gilbreath, GetInTheLoop Labs
**Subject:** Data access inquiry — compact membership and privilege-fee data

---

Hello,

I'm building PT PracticePath, a tool that helps PTs and PTAs choose their
fastest path to practice in a new state. For a large share of our users the
right answer is a compact privilege — so the compact's data is central to
what we show: which jurisdictions are actively issuing and accepting,
per-state privilege fees, and jurisprudence prerequisites.

Today we cite ptcompact.org as the authoritative source for all of it, but
we can't verify programmatically: the compact map and the privilege
fee/jurisprudence chart aren't reachable by automated access (nor indexed in
a machine-usable form). In practice this means the most decision-relevant
numbers in our product — privilege fees — are the hardest to keep verifiably
current, and we currently omit them rather than rely on secondary sources.

Is there a supported channel — a data file, feed, or API — for membership
status, per-state fees, and jurisprudence requirements? Even a periodically
published CSV would let us present compact privileges as accurately as they
deserve, and we'd of course link users to ptcompact.org to purchase.

We'd also gladly share back what we observe (e.g., member-state pages whose
compact information disagrees with the map — we flagged one such conflict in
our research).

Who would be the right contact for this?

Thank you,
Jason Gilbreath
