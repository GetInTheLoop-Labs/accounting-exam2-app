# Outreach draft — FSBPT (data access / partnership)

**To:** FSBPT (fsbpt.org — start via the general contact form or
Free Resources / Licensure Reference Guide team)
**From:** Jason Gilbreath, GetInTheLoop Labs
**Subject:** Data collaboration inquiry — practitioner-facing licensure guidance tool

---

Hello,

I'm preparing to launch PT PracticePath as a beta for physical therapists
and PTAs. It helps them understand everything required to practice in a
given position and state — licensure path comparison (examination,
endorsement, compact privilege), per-requirement costs and current
processing times, and setting-specific requirements — with every fact cited
to its official source and stamped with when it was verified.

We're reaching out before the beta opens because accuracy is the product's
entire premise, and we'd rather build on a supported data channel from day
one than scrape around one.

FSBPT's resources are the authoritative national layer of that picture: the
NPTE (fees, fixed dates, registration and jurisdiction-approval flow), the
Score Transfer Service, the Coursework Tools for foreign-educated
applicants, and the Jurisdiction Licensure Reference Guide.

Two things we'd value discussing:

1. **Programmatic access.** Is there a supported way to consume LRG
   jurisdiction data and current fee/date facts programmatically (an API,
   data files, or a licensing arrangement)? Today we verify against your
   public pages; automated access to fsbpt.org is bot-blocked, which we
   respect — we would far rather consume a supported channel at whatever
   cadence you consider appropriate.

2. **Accuracy.** Our product's core promise is that stale information is
   flagged, never presented as current. A supported data relationship with
   FSBPT directly improves what practitioners see. We're equally happy to
   share back anything useful — e.g., we detect fee/rule changes across all
   53 jurisdiction boards as part of our verification sweeps.

Happy to share a demo. Who would be the right person to speak with about
data access?

Thank you,
Jason Gilbreath
