## 2. What We Know Going In (from the July 6 internal call)

- Board-level theory of the problem: high churn driven by weak **onboarding and early-tenure supervision**; "bad unit economics"; high variability across ~16 states.
- The lead arrived pre-solved: Steve described actAVA's template HR onboarding agents "to a T."
- Nate's stated goal for the call: broaden from the agent solution into the data/governance/readiness conversation and gauge where Amy's head is at.

**The sharper angle to hold quietly:** for the ABA line of business, early-tenure supervision is not just a retention lever — **RBT supervision is a BACB compliance obligation** (required percentage of supervised hours, documented), layered over state-by-state licensure variation across 16 states. If early supervision is thin, Clarvida likely has a compliance exposure and a retention problem wearing the same clothes. Agent-assisted supervision scheduling/documentation — freeing scarce BCBA hours for actual supervision — is both the better framing and a naturally contained first workflow.

## 3. Listening Framework → Proposal Refinement Map

| # | Listen for | Refines in the proposal |

| 1 | **Board theory vs. operator pain.** Do Amy/Jen independently name churn/onboarding — or something else (documentation burden, EHR adoption, intake, scheduling, credentialing)? | Whether the proposal leads with the onboarding workflow or leads with scored workflow selection (Phase 1 earns its place if their pain differs from Steve's theory). |

| 6 | **Decision path & money.** Who else says yes; budget vehicle (transformation vs. HR); their clock. | Pricing tier; whether entry is a small diagnostic or a fuller track. |

## 4. If Invited to Speak — Three Questions That Demo the Method

1. *"What do you actually see in the data about when people leave — is it a 90-day cliff, and does it look the same across your 16 states?"* (baseline thinking)
2. *"If early attrition dropped ten points, where would it show up financially — recruiting spend, billable hours, caseload coverage?"* (business value levers)
3. *"Who owns onboarding today — one process with local flavor, or sixteen processes?"* (containment/standardization — Amy's language)

## 5. Watch-Fors

- **The activation-first trap, live.** The lead arrived pre-solved. If the call races toward agent demos, the refinement insight is that the proposal must insert discovery *before* the build. Don't fight it in the room; note it.
- **Tasked vs. bought-in.** Ownership language ("we've been struggling with…") vs. delegation language ("Steve asked us to look at…"). Executive-alignment content matters far more in the second case.
- **Transformation-office framing is the friend.** These buyers think in operating models, standardization, governance — write the Clarvida proposal to a CTO, not a CHRO.
- **No commitments in the room.** Scope, price, timeline, build stay open — nothing is papered until the Empactful/GITLAI working model is set. Suppress brainstorm-mode generosity.
- **This call is platform R&D.** It's a live specimen of the discovery/intake instrument — note what an interview agent should have captured before this meeting; feed the capture log.
